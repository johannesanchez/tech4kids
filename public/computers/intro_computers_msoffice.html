Learn the Basics of Google Docs and Google Sheets

Introduction to Google Docs

Google Docs is an online word processor that allows you to create and edit text documents collaboratively. It is similar to Microsoft Word and is completely free to use.

Learning Objectives

  • Create a new document
  • Format text and paragraphs
  • Insert images, tables, and links
  • Collaborate with others in real-time

    • Getting Started

    1. Go to Google Docs.
    2. Log in with your Google account.
    3. Click on Blank to create a new document.

Introduction to Google Sheets

Google Sheets is an online spreadsheet application that allows you to create, edit, and share spreadsheets. It is similar to Microsoft Excel and is completely free to use.

Learning Objectives

  • Create a new spreadsheet
  • Enter and format data
  • Perform basic calculations
  • Create simple charts

Getting Started

  1. Go to Google Sheets.
  2. Log in with your Google account.
  3. Click on Blank to create a new spreadsheet.

Introduction to Google Drive

Google Drive is an online file storage and synchronization service. It allows you to store your files in the cloud and access them from any device.

Learning Objectives

  • Upload and organize files
  • Share files and folders with others
  • Create new Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides directly from Google Drive
  • Collaborate on files in real-time

Getting Started

  1. Go to Google Drive.
  2. Log in with your Google account.
  3. Click on the + New button to create or upload files.
  • Upload a document, image, or video to Google Drive.
  • Create a folder and move your uploaded file into the folder.
  • Share the folder with a classmate or your instructor and set the sharing permission to "View" or "Edit".
  • Open a shared document and add comments or suggestions.
